Carbonara

Description

Carbonara builds on top of Pasta alla Gricia by incoporating eggs into the recipe to build a creamy pasta sauce.

Ingredients

  • A large glass or metal bowl that would fit on top of the pot used to boil pasta
  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 pound guanciale or pancetta, thickly cubed
  • 1/4 cup Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan, finely grated
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon black pepper, grounded
  • 4 eggs

Additionally, reserve:

  • 1-2 cup of pasta water
  • A few tablespoons of the rendered fat from the guanciale or pancetta

Steps

  1. Cut guanciale or pancetta into thick cubes.
  2. In the large glass or metal bowl (the one that can fit on top of the pasta pot), combine the cheese, black pepper, and eggs. Mix until it reaches a paste-like consistency.
  3. Fill a pot with water enough to cover at least half the spaghetti.
  4. Add salt to the water and get it boiling.
  5. Add your meat onto a cold pan, and set it to medium-low heat to render the fat. Let the meat crisp up and get a little browning.
  6. Once the meat is crisped up and starts browning a little, use a spatual to push the meat onto one side of the pan seperating it from the rendered fat. Turn the heat off.
  7. Drain the rendered fat into a smaller bowl. Reserve a few tablespoons of the rendered fat.
  8. Once the pasta is cooked to al dente, transfer from pot to pan. Reserve 1-2 cups of pasta water. Mix the pasta with the cooked pork and any remaining rendered fat left in the pan.
  9. Do not pour away the pasta water. In the pot with the pasta water, turn the heat down to low, but large bowl on top.
  10. Work quickly! Transfer pasta with pork from pan into the large bowl. Combine pasta with paste-like mixture by mixing quickly. The paste should start to emulsify into a sauce. Add in bits of pasta water but keep mixing.
  11. If you've done it right, the eggs should not be cooked like scrambled eggs, but instead a glossy sauce.
  12. Transfer pasta to a plate or bowl.
  13. Garnish with freshly cracked black pepper and some extra cheese.

Enjoy!
